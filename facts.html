Smoking: the leading cause of preventable diseases and death worldwide.

  • Despite the life threatening consequences of smoking,
    over 1 in 5 Dutch citizens are a smoker.
  • Every year, 6 million deaths a year are caused worldwide by tobacco use, and the expectation is that this number will have grown to 8 million by 2030.
  • Smokers die approximately 10 years earlier than than non-smokers.
  • The tobacco industry spent $9.5 billion in 2016 on advertising and promotion of cigarettes and tobacco. This is equivalent to $26 million per day, and more than $1 million per hour.
  • It is estimated that every day, over 3200 people aged under 18 smoke their first cigarette.
  • Based on research from 2015, almost 7 out of 10 adult smokers wanted to quit.
